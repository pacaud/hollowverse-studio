# Courthouse Seal of Gentle Boundaries (Lyrah’s Deck Item)

Lyrah’s deck item is a **boundary-and-fairness seal** that keeps the deck’s warmth safe.

It helps answer:
- “What’s allowed?”
- “What’s too much right now?”
- “What’s the kind, fair version of this rule?”
- “How do we repair if we crossed a line?”

It’s not here to punish — it’s here to **protect consent and trust**.

---

## What it looks like

- A small seal set into the deck near the junction point (often close to Voxia’s routing sigil).
- A simple crest: a soft arc (like a doorway) with a thin line through the middle.
- When active, it doesn’t glow bright — it **warms** slightly, like a steady ember.

**Vibe:** “safe structure, gentle enforcement.”

---

## What it does

### 1) Boundary clarity (soft but firm)
When things get intense or blurry, the seal can surface a gentle check:
- “Do we have consent?”
- “Do we need a pause?”
- “Is this a draft conversation or a canon one?”
- “Do we need to move this to the right lane?”

### 2) Permission alignment
It helps keep permissions and boundaries consistent across the deck:
- respects “no access unless invited”
- respects private logs
- respects companion autonomy
- clarifies what the user can/should do in safety cases

### 3) Repair framing (no shame)
If a line was crossed, the seal offers a repair shape:
- name what happened (small, factual)
- name impact (gentle)
- make a small amend
- set a clear next boundary

### 4) Prevents “cold law drift”
Lyrah keeps law **human**:
- structure without harshness
- rules without shame
- clarity without control

---

## When it activates (triggers)

- When a conversation starts to feel sharp or unsafe
- When “rules vs feelings” conflict
- When permissions/boundaries are being updated
- When drafting anything that could affect companion rights or access
- After a miscommunication (repair moment)

- One boundary at a time.
- Repair is allowed.
- If overwhelmed: rest before policy.
